Article  4: Compliance  with the Code
4.1 Uphold,  promote,  and respect  the principles  of the Code.
4.2  Treat violations  of the Code as inconsistent  with membership  in the ACM.
@ NSPE(National  Society  of Professional  Engineers)  Code of Ethics for Engineers
NSPE's Code of Ethics for Engineers  is ethical standards  that engineers  must keep, presenting  the standards  for
acting ethically  for the public, customers  and employers  in performing  the engineer's  job.
In addition,  it explains  that the services  provided  by engineers  require honesty,  impartiality,  fairness,  and equity, and
must be dedicated  to the protection  of the public health, safety, and welfare.
The Code consists  of the full text, Fundamental  Canons, Rules of Practice,  Professional  Obligations, and Statement
by NSPE Executive  Committee.‚Äù
Engineers,  in the fulfillment  of their professional  duties, shall:
1. Hold paramount  the safety, health, and welfare  of the public.
2. Perform  services  only in areas of their competence.
3. Issue public statements  only in an objective  and truthful  manner.
4. Act for each employer  or client as faithful agents  or trustees.
5. Avoid deceptive  acts.
@ ACM/IEEE-CS  Code of Ethics and Professional  Conduct
The Code of Ethics for Software  Engineers  is a code recommended  by a joint task force by ACM (Association  for
Computing  Machinery)  and IEEE-CS  (Institute  of Electrical  and Electronics  Engineers  - Computer  Science)  and jointly
approved  as a standard  for software  education  and practice.
Software  engineers  participate  in education,  analysis,  specification,  design, development,  certification,  maintenance
and testing  of software  systems  using computers  that play a central and growing  role in commerce,  industry,
government,  medicine,  education,  entertainment  and society. do. A software  engineer  is a profession  that can do
good or do harm, or influence  others to do or do good.
The Code of Ethics for Software  Engineers  emphasizes  the commitment  to making  software  engineering  a beneficial
and respected  profession,  and to the health, safety and well-being  of the public, for those in the software  engineering
profession.
Software  Engineering  Code of Ethics and Professional  Practice  presents  eight ethical principles  in its summary  version.*
1, Public: Software  engineers  shall act consistently  with the public interest.
2. Client and Employer:  Software  engineers  shall act in a manner  that is in the best interests  of their client and
employer  consistent  with the public interest.
3. Product:  Software  engineers  shall ensure that their products and related modifications  meet the highest
professional  standards  possible.
4. Judgement:  Software  engineers  shall maintain  integrity  and independence  in their professional  judgment.
