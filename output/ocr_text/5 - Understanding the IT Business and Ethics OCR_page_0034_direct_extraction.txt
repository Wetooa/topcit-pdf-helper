C) Strategy  setting  process
The strategy-setting  process  consists  of strategy  analysis,  strategy  selection,  strategy  implementation  and
management,  and feedback.  First, in the strategy  analysis  phase, basic information  regarding  the company  is
investigated,  and the As-Is model is studied.  In the strategy  selection  phase, the future (To-Be) model and the
strategy  for realizing  the To-Be model are established.  In the strategy  implementation  and management  phase, the
strategy  is adopted  and put into effect. When necessary,  adjustments  can be made to a strategic plan during this
phase.

@ Strategy  analysis
The purpose  of the strategy  analysis is to collect  the information  required to make the business  decisions  of the
company.  The internal environment  can be improved  because  it can be controlled  by the company,  whereas  the
external  environment  is generally  difficult  to control and requires  an adaptive  strategy.
+ External  environment  analysis
This is an analysis  of the environment  surrounding  a company  and other external  conditions.
- (Macro-environment)  This is the political,  economic,  social and technological  environment  surrounding  a company.
The boundaries  of its influence  can be expanded  across several industries.
- (Micro-environment)  As the industrial  environment  to which a company  belongs,  the micro-environment  is where
the company  faces competition  when acquiring  resources  and selling  its products/services,  and is what we often call
a general  competitive  environment.
+ Internal  environment  analysis
The analysis  of a company’s  internal environment  focuses  on internal elements  and processes,  resources,
organizations,  etc., and also focuses  on the internal  changes  taking place within a company.
+ Consolidated  environment  analysis
- A comprehensive  analysis  of the external  and internal environmental  factors of a company  is carried out, and
integrated  internal/external  analysis  results can be obtained.
- SWOT analysis  is an important  tool of consolidated  environment  analysis.  A SWOT analysis  examines  the strengths
(S) and weaknesses  (W) of a company’s  internal  environment  and the opportunities  (O) and threats  (T) present  in its
external  environment.
