01 What is the Protocol?
<Table 1> above showed many standard  protocols,  such as ARP, IP TCP, HTTP, DNS, and NAT. A protocol is a
standardized  communication  rule for sending  and receiving  data through  a network. The Internet,  as we know, is a
large group of networks  that connect  multiple  PCs and servers.
For tens or hundreds  of thousands  of PCs, servers, and routers that connect  them to exchange  data, it is necessary
to have a common  protocol  that can be found by each other, and there are several international  organizations  that
standardize  these protocols.
+ IETF (Internet  Engineering  Task Force) is responsible  for most Internet-related  standard  protocols.
+ 3GPP (Third Generation  Partnership  Project)  and 3GPP2 are responsible  for wireless  communication  protocols  such
as GSM, CDMA, UMTS, LTE, and LTE-A.
+ ITU-T (International  Telecommunication  Union Telecommunication  Standardization  Sector) is responsible  for
protocols  related to telephones.
Therefore,  it is necessary  to have the competitiveness  to understand  and implement  (if necessary)  the standard
protocols  enacted  by these standardization  organizations  to develop  software  in the network/communication  sector.
