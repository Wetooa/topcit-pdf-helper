B) Technical  trends  of embedded  hardware
@ Expanded  connectivity
Studies  of applying  connectivity  technologies,  such as WiFi, LTE, BLE, and ZigBee, used by mobile devices and
servers/PCs,  to embedded  systems,  have increased  with the convergence  of the IT industry.  Traditional  industries  and
interoperability  between  devices,  such as loT and M2M sensor  networks,  have also become  important.
@ Low-power  32-bit processor
Processors  for embedded  systems  were mostly converted  from 8 bits to 32 bits. As such, the number  of
embedded  systems  using an OS has rapidly increased,  and the portion of general-purpose  OS, like Linux, has
significantlyincreased.

Embedded  operating  systems,  supporting  wearables  and loT devices, require lighter weight and lower power
consumption,  while operating  systems  applied to conventional  embedded  devices  include middleware,  related to high
functionality,  and stability  and convenience  functions  like 3D acceleration  GUI and multimedia.  As a result, technology
polarization  is accelerating.
