C) Foreign  policy related  to personal  information  protection
@ OECD Privacy  Guidelines®
The OECD Privacy Guidelines  is named the Recommendation  Concerning  Guidelines Governing  the Protection
of Privacy and Transborder  Flows of Personal  Data, which were established  in 1980 as guidelines  for the cross-
border transfer  of personal  data in 1980. Since then, it has been revised to reflect the opinions  of the international
community  on the collection  and management  of personal  information,  and is currently  used as basic data for the
development  of laws and guidelines  related to personal  information  in countries  around the world.
Collection + There should be limits to the collection  of personal  data and any such data should be obtained  by
Limitation  Principle lawful and fair means and, where appropriate,  with the consent  of the data subject.
Data Quality + Personal  data should be relevant  to the purposes  for which they are to be used, and, to the extent
Principle necessary  for those purposes,  should be accurate,  complete  and kept up to date.
