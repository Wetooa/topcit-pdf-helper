B) Characteristics  of embedded  software
Embedded  software  is embedded  in a physical product  and continuously interacts  with the physical  environment.
In other words, it must continuously  receive and process signals from the physical  environment.  In some cases,
it is necessary  to receive the userâ€™s requirements  and respond  to them, by considering  changes  in the physical
environment.  There are also cases, such as computers  and smartphones,  where it only responds  to user requirements.
Therefore,  embedded  software  has characteristics,  such as reactivity,  timeliness,  concurrency,  heterogeneity,  liveness,
resource  and environmental  constraints,  and determinism.  <Table 21> below shows features  of each characteristic.
