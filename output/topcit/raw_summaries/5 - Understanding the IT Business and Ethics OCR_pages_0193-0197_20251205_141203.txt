## Personal Information Protection: Learning Guide

### 1. Key Concepts & Definitions

*   **Sensitive Information:** A broad category of personal data including ideas, beliefs, trade union/political party membership, political opinions, health information, genetic information, criminal history, etc.
*   **Unique Identification Information:** Data used to identify an individual, such as resident registration number, passport number, driver's license number, or alien registration number.
*   **Personal Information Impact Assessment (PIIA):** An evaluation system to analyze risks and find improvements when new personal information files are introduced or existing ones modified, especially if infringement concerns exist.
*   **Image Information Processing Device:** A device (e.g., CCTV) installed in a space to capture or transmit images of people or objects. Operators must establish and disclose a policy for its operation and management.

### 2. Personal Information Management Areas (Checklist Summary)

Public institutions (and generally all organizations) should ensure the following:

*   **Personal Information Impact Assessment (PIIA):**
    *   Conduct PIIAs when information is subject to assessment.
    *   Establish and implement a PIIA plan.
*   **Image Information Processing Device Protection:**
    *   Establish and disclose operational and management policies, reflecting all legal obligations.
    *   Record and manage use, provision, access, and destruction of personal image information in a ledger.
        *   **Required Register Items:** Classification (use, provision, reading, destruction), date/time, file name/type, person in charge, purpose/reason, third party for data use/provision/reading, grounds for use/provision, type of use/provision, period.
*   **Leakage and Exposure Prevention:**
    *   Operate systems to prevent leakage on webpages and personal information processing systems.
    *   Operate systems to prevent leakage on business computers and mobile devices.
*   **Infringement & Emergency Response:**
    *   Establish and disseminate procedures for responding to personal information infringement incidents.
    *   Establish and disseminate emergency response procedures.
*   **Personal Information Processing System Management:**
    *   Establish and implement policies for managing access rights.
    *   Perform checks and follow-up measures for access records.
*   **Unique Identification Information Restrictions:**
    *   Understand the processing status of unique identification information.
    *   Implement encryption for unique identification information.

### 4. International Personal Information Protection Policies

#### A. OECD Privacy Guidelines

*   **Official Name:** Recommendation Concerning Guidelines Governing the Protection of Privacy and Transborder Flows of Personal Data.
*   **Purpose:** Established in 1980 as guidelines for cross-border transfer of personal data; now a global standard for developing personal information laws.
*   **Core Principles:**
    1.  **Collection Limitation:** Limits on data collection; data must be obtained lawfully, fairly, and with consent (where appropriate).
    2.  **Data Quality:** Personal data should be relevant, accurate, complete, and up-to-date for its intended use.
    3.  **Purpose Specification:** Purposes for data collection must be specified; subsequent use limited to those purposes.
    4.  **Use Limitation:** Data should not be disclosed or used for unspecified purposes without consent or legal authority.
    5.  **Security Safeguards:** Reasonable security measures must protect data against unauthorized access, destruction, modification, etc.
    6.  **Openness:** General policy of transparency regarding personal data; means to identify data controller readily available.
    7.  **Individual Participation:** Individuals have the right to confirm, erase, rectify, or amend their data.
    8.  **Accountability:** Data controller is accountable for complying with these principles.

#### B. EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

*   **What it is:** A unified rule by the European Parliament to strengthen the privacy of EU citizens.
*   **Timeline:** Promulgated in 2016, effective May 25, 2018.
*   **Scope & Compliance:**
    *   Applies to all companies processing personal information of EU citizens.
    *   Even companies without an EU establishment must comply if they offer goods/services to EU residents.
    *   Violations require notification to the supervisory authority within 72 hours.
*   **Main Contents:**
    *   Expands application to all companies processing personal information.
    *   Enhances data subjects' rights.
    *   Strengthens data controllers' responsibilities.
    *   Generally prohibits international transfer of personal information abroad.
    *   Requires privacy protection from the processing design stage ("privacy-by-design").
*   **Key Entities in GDPR:**
    *   **Data Subject:** The 'living natural person' who creates personal information and holds rights to it.
    *   **Controller:** Responsible for processing personal information; determines purpose and means of processing.
    *   **Processor:** An individual, corporation, or public institution entrusted by the Controller to process personal information according to the Controller's directions.
*   **Enhanced Data Subject Rights:**
    1.  **Right to be Informed:** Individuals must be informed about data collection and use.
    2.  **Right of Access:** Individuals can access their personal data.
    3.  **Right to Rectification:** Individuals can have inaccurate data corrected.
    4.  **Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten):** Individuals can request their data be deleted under certain conditions.
    5.  **Right to Restrict Processing:** Individuals can limit how their data is used.
    6.  **Right to Data Portability:** Individuals can obtain and reuse their personal data across different services.
    7.  **Right to Object:** Individuals can object to certain types of data processing.
    8.  **Rights in relation to Automated Decision Making and Profiling:** Individuals have rights regarding decisions made solely by automated means (e.g., marketing profiling based on hobbies/location) and must be notified and allowed to decide if their information is used.
*   **GDPR Penalty Calculation Criteria:**
    *   Factors considered include: nature, gravity, and duration of infringement; intentional/negligent character; mitigation actions; controller/processor responsibility; previous infringements; cooperation with authority; categories of data affected; notification; supervisory authority measures; adherence to codes; financial benefits gained/losses avoided.
    *   **Maximum Fines:** Up to 20 million EUR, or 4% of the total worldwide annual turnover of the preceding financial year (whichever is higher) for an undertaking.

#### C. US Consumer Privacy Bill of Rights

*   **Core Principles:**
    *   **Individual Control:** Right of the data subject to control their personal information.
    *   **Transparency:** Transparency in accessing information.
    *   **Respect for Context:** Right to know the context and reason for data collection, use, and disclosure.
    *   **Security:** Strengthened security for personal information.
    *   **Access and Accuracy:** Right to access personal information and ensure its accuracy.
    *   **Focused Collection:** Limits on personal information collected and held by businesses.
    *   **Accountability:** Data collecting agencies must comply with legislation and act accordingly.

### 5. Proactive Measures for Personal Information Controllers

*   **Technical/Administrative Protection:** Implement robust technical and administrative safeguards for personal information.
