@ Characteristics  of the EDW
The EDW was created  with a clear purpose  for its use. The concept  explains  a very import characteristic  of the EDW,
amely, that of being topic-centered.  Upon adopting  the EDW, critical data within and outside  the company  become
concentrated  and consolidated  in one place; and data are loaded and used in a time-based  order. Once stored, data
will not be destroyed  or changed  unless there is a particular  reason for doing so. These characteristics  of the EDW are
described  in detail as follows.5

@ Configuration  of the EDW
The EDW is configured  so that the work support  system collects, transforms  and loads data during operations,
analyzes  data that meet the given requirements,  and visualizes  them in such a way that they will be of assistance
when making  decisions.  From a macro perspective,  all these processes  are referred  to as the EDW, and from a
microscopic  point of view, the repository  for storing  data is referred  to as the EDW.
