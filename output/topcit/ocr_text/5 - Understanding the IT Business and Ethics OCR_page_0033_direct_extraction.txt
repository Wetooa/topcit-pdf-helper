ESSENCE
+ Mission
A company’s  mission  is typically  either its objective  or its main business.  A mission  describes  what an entity does for
its own benefit  and is not bound by time.
+ Vision
A company’s  vision is the ideal future that it aspires  to. A vision can only be obtained  by the successful  execution  of
the mission.  A company's  vision is both a source of inspiration  and an extension  of its capabilities.
* Goals
Goals must be measurable,  high-level,  and support  an entity in its efforts  to achieve  its mission.
+ Objectives
Specific,  quantifiable  and low-level  targets  which signal that the objectives  have been reached.
+ Principles
Principles  state the constraints  that a company  must consider  when selecting  the method of achieving  its targets.
The basic ideology  of a company  represents  its core values, and is used in shaping  its strategy.
+ Enablers
Enablers  are the external  environments  and corporate  strengths  that can boost a company’s  ability to achieve its
goals and objectives.
+ Barriers
Barriers  are the external  environments  and corporate  weaknesses  that impede  an entity's  ability to achieve  its goals
and objectives.
+ Strategy
Strategy  is the approach  a company  takes to realize its mission, objectives,  and goals. Strategy  must support  a
company’s  vision, but it is affected  by enablers,  barriers, and the basic ideology.
+ Strategic  plan
A strategic  plan is a document  created  through  strategic  plan setting  activities.  A strategic  plan explains  the strategy
of a company  and the factors  that affect the strategy.
+ Initiatives
Initiatives  are a set of activities  carried out to implement  a strategy.
+ Actions
Actions  are specific  procedures  for achieving  goals and objectives.  Generally  speaking,  people are assigned  to manage
activities,  and activities  are subject  to certain  timetable  constraints.
