@ Works Made for Hire
The term “work made for hire” means a work made by an employee  of a legal person, organization  or other employers
(hereinafter  referred  to as ‘legal person, etc.” during the course of his or her duties and on the initiative  of legal
person, etc”,
The authorship  of a work made for hire which is made public under the name of such a legal person,  etc. as the author
shall be attributed  to that legal person, etc., unless otherwise  stipulated  in the contract  or work regulation,  etc®.
In order for a legal person, etc. to become  the author of a work made for hire, it must meet all of the following
requirements  (Korea Copyright  Commission's  Consultation  Case Book)
+ The work must be initiated by a legal person  or the like.
+ It must be written by a person who is engaged  in business  such as a legal person.
+ It must be a work written  for business.
+ It must be publicized  in the name of a legal person,  etc.
+ There must be no other things set in the contract  or work rules.
