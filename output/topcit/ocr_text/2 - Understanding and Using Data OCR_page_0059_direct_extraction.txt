06 Another  Type of ERD Notation:  The Crow’s  Foot Model
Chen's ERD (described  above) is mainly used in the academic  world, whereas  information  engineering  notation,  also
known as Crow’s Foot notation,  is mainly used in industry.
A) Entity, attribute,  identifier
© Specify  the entity name outside  the rectangle.
@ Specify  the attribute  name inside the rectangle.
© The rectangle  is divided into top/bottom.  Specify  the identifier  attribute  at the top and the general attributes  at the
bottom.

B) Characteristics  of the entity
@ Information  must be required  by the work concerned  and should be the information  to be managed. (Example:
Patient,  number  of times TOEIC exams are taken, etc.)
@ An entity should be identifiable  by a unique identifier.
@ Anentity  should be a set of instances  that exist permanently.
(Not “one”, but “two or more’)
@ Anentity  should be used by the work process.
© Anentity  must have an attribute.
© An entity should have at least one relationship  with other entities.
C) Classification  of entities
@ Classification  by tangible  and intangible  type - Generally,  entities  can be classified  into tangible  entities,  concept
entities, and event entities  depending  on whether  they are tangible  or intangible.
Classification  by the time of occurrence  - Entities can be classified  into fundamental  entities/key  entities, main
entities, and active entities  depending  on the time of occurrence.
