03 Understanding  of Industrial  Property  Rights and New Intellectual
Property  Rights
A) Concept  of Industrial  Property  Rights
@ Definition  and Types of Industrial  Property  Rights
Industrial  property  rights are to protect any invention  that has commercial  value, and mainly  refer to patent rights,
utility model rights, design rights, and trademark  rights.
<Table 110> Industrial  Property  Rights by Type
Classification Content
+ Right to an advanced  invention
+ Methods  such as of manufacturing,  production,  etc. are subject  to patent  application  only
+ Inventions  that were publicly  known or performed,  or published  in publications,  or made available  to the
public through  telecommunication  networks  at home and abroad prior to filing a patent, are  not subject
to patent  application.
+ The duration  of the patent right is 20 years from the date of the patent applicationPatents
+ Right to a mark used to identify  one's own products  (services  or products  related  to the provision  of
services)  from those of another.
+ Mark: Any mark that displays  a product  with symbols,  letters,  figures,  sounds,  smells, holograms,
movements,  or colors. Trade names can also be registered  as trademarks
Trademarks + Service mark: a trademark  used in connection  with services,  such as academies,  restaurants,  etc. The
domain  of a website  can also be registered  as a service mark
+ Aservice  mark (trademark)  takes precedence  over the registered  domain  of a website.
+ Trademark  rights and service  mark rights can be used virtually  permanently  by registering  for renewal
every 10 years.
