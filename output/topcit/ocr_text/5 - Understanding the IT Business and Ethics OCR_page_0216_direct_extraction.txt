B) Concept  of Industrial  Property  Rights
@ Definition  and Types of Industrial  Property  Rights
Intellectual  property  rights related to computer  programs,  genetically  engineered  plants and animals,  semiconductor
design, the Internet,  and animated  characters,  which are difficult  to protect  with traditional  intellectual  property  rights
such as patent  rights and copyrights,  are called new intellectual  property  rights.
New intellectual  property  rights are of the following  types.
+ Industrial  copyright:  computer  program,  artificial  intelligence,  database,  etc.
+ High-tech  industrial  property  rights: semiconductor  integrated  circuit  layout design, biotechnology  field
+ Information  property  rights: trade secrets,  multimedia,  etc.
The World Trade Organization  WTO covers the scope of new intellectual  property  rights in literary, artistic and
scientific  works, albums and broadcasts  of directors  and artists, inventions  in all fields of human endeavors,  scientific
inventions,  industrial  designs, registered  trademarks,  service marks, trade names and other names, rights under
protection  against unfair competition,  and all other rights arising from intellectual  activities  in the fields of industry,
science,  literature  or art.

A) Concept  of Unfair Competition  Prevention
@® Concept  of Unfair  Competition  Prevention
“Unfair  Competition  Prevention  and Trade Secret Protection’  is to prevent  acts of unfair competition  such as improper
use of domestically  well-known  trademarks  and trade names, and by preventing  infringement  of trade secrets.
The term “act of unfair competition”  means any of the following  acts:
+ using marks identical  or similar  to, another  person's  name, trade name, trademark,  or container  or package  of goods
or any other mark* indicating  another  person's  goods, which is widely known in the Republic  of Korea; or by selling,
distributing,  importing,  or exporting  goods bearing  such marks; or selling, distributing,  importing,  or exporting  goods
bearing  such marks
+ An act of making false marks of the place of origin on goods, or on trade documents  or in communications  by
means of advertisements  of the goods or in a manner  that makes the public aware of the marks: or by selling,
distributing,  importing,  or exporting  goods bearing  such marks
+ An act of falsely assuming  another  person's  goods or an act of advertising  any goods or making a mark in any
manner  of leading  the public to misunderstand  their quality, content,  manufacturing  process,  usage, or quantity,  in
latter goods or advertisement  thereof, or selling, distributing,  importing,  or exporting  goods using such method  or
mark;
