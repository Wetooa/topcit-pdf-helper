B) Framework  of IT governance
Due to the alignment  of IT with the business  goals of a company,  in IT governance,  the value of IT contributes  to the
attainment  of good business  performance  and results. Through  the management  of risks and core IT resources  for
the efficient  operation  of IT, the roles and responsibilities  related to IT resources  are clearly defined. IT governance  is
structured  to ensure reliability  and transparency.
The following  figure shows the governance  framework  reference  model of COBIT 5 (Control Objects  for Information
and Related  Technology  5). This framework  is widely known as the “IT governance  framework”.  In the reference  model,
the governance  and management  processes  of a company  are kept separate.

@ Governance  area
The governance  area consists  of 5 processes  (evaluation  (E), directive  (D), monitoring  (M), etc.), each of which comes
with its own work procedures.
+ EDM 1. Establish  the measurement  standard  with the governance  framework.
+ EDM 2. Provide  profits.
+ EDM 3. Minimize  risks.
+ EDM 4. Optimize  risks.
+ EDM 5. Ensure the transparency  of stakeholders.
@ Management  area
The management  are consists  of 4 process phases:  planning  (APO), deployment  (BAI), operation  (DSS), monitoring
(MEA)
+ Planning  (APO) phase: linking, planning
+ Deployment  (BAI) phase: deploying,  acquiring,  configuring
+ Operation  (DSS)phase:  transferring,  service,  support
+ Monitoring  (MEA)phase:  monitoring,  evaluations,  review
