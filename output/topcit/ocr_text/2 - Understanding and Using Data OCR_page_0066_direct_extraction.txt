09 Integrity  and Key
Data integrity refers to the need to protect data from lost updates  of data to maintain  their accuracy,  validity,
consistency,  and stability.  Three to five types of integrity  are defined depending  on the viewpoint.  Three types of
integrity  include entity, referential,  and domain integrity.  Five types of integrity  are also defined by separating  entity
integrity  into entity integrity  and key integrity,  and by including  user-defined  integrity.  To main integrity  in this way,
the primary  and foreign keys should be carefully  selected  and used.

value, etc. can be defined  and used. The attribute  value has atomicity  and should be a value defined  in the domain.
@ Key integrity:  All records  in a table should be identifiable  from each other.
